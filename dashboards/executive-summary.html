Where Vulnerability Converges

This composite index combines food insecurity rate (40%), poverty rate (30%), and relative meal cost (30%) into a single score per state. States scoring highest face the triple burden of high hunger, deep poverty, and expensive food.

Grant writers can use this single metric to identify where need is greatest and funding most impactful.

Sources USDA ERS, Feeding America, U.S. Census Bureau ACS
Data Year 2022
Index Formula (Insecurity × 0.4) + (Poverty × 0.3) + (Meal Cost × 0.3)

SNAP Coverage Gap

Is SNAP Reaching the Hungry?

For the 15 most food-insecure states, this chart compares the food-insecure population against SNAP participants. Where SNAP bars fall short of insecurity bars, a coverage gap exists — meaning eligible families are not receiving benefits.

States with strong outreach programs show SNAP coverage meeting or exceeding food-insecure populations. The gap reveals where enrollment efforts and policy changes can make the biggest impact.

Sources USDA FNS SNAP Data Tables & Feeding America Map the Meal Gap 2024
Data Year 2022

Food Price Impact

Grocery Inflation Over Time

Year-over-year change in the BLS Food at Home Consumer Price Index. The 2022 spike — peaking above 13% — was the worst grocery inflation in over 40 years. Though it has cooled, prices remain elevated, squeezing families already on the edge.

For food banks, this means every dollar buys less food. For families, it means the gap between income and an adequate diet keeps widening.

Source Bureau of Labor Statistics, Consumer Price Index
Updated Monthly (BLS API)
Calculation (Current CPI - CPI 12 months prior) / CPI 12 months prior × 100

Vulnerability Index

The composite vulnerability index is computed per state as: (food insecurity rate × 0.4) + (poverty rate × 0.3) + (normalized meal cost × 30). Meal cost is normalized against the national maximum ($5.12 in Hawaii). Higher scores indicate greater combined vulnerability across all three dimensions.

SNAP Coverage Gap

The SNAP coverage gap compares food-insecure persons (from Feeding America) against SNAP participants (from USDA FNS administrative data). A gap exists when food-insecure persons exceed SNAP participants, indicating eligible families who are not enrolled.

Data Currency

Food insecurity and poverty rates reflect 2022. BLS food prices update monthly via live API. SNAP participation uses FY2022-2023 administrative data. The USDA ERS food security report was discontinued after 2024; Feeding America continues annually.
